Leadership & Faith

Leading with
purpose and conviction

On the intersection of faith, character, and what it means to truly lead people well.

Core principles

What shapes how I lead

These aren't just professional values — they're convictions formed over years of faith, relationships, and learning from failure.

01

Servant leadership

Leadership is not about position or title — it's about service. The leaders I most admire are those who make others around them more capable, more confident, and more themselves. That is the standard I hold myself to.

02

Integrity over comfort

Faith teaches that doing the right thing and doing the easy thing are often very different paths. In security, in business, and in life — I would rather have a hard conversation now than compromise on what I believe to be right.

03

People before outcomes

Results matter — but the way we treat people on the way to those results matters more. Sustainable success is built on trust, genuine care, and environments where people feel truly seen and valued.

My perspective

Faith as a foundation for leadership

I've spent a lot of time thinking about what makes someone a good leader. The answers I've found most compelling aren't in management frameworks or business literature — though those have their place. They come from a deeper place: from faith, from community, from asking honest questions about purpose and character.

"The measure of a leader is not in the authority they hold, but in the dignity they extend to those they lead."

For me, faith isn't separate from work. It's the lens through which I see it. It shapes how I think about responsibility — not just to shareholders or clients, but to the people on my team whose livelihoods, growth, and wellbeing are in part shaped by the decisions I make each day. That's a weight I take seriously.

In cybersecurity, we deal with real-world consequences. Decisions made under pressure, with incomplete information, often carrying significant risk. Having a stable foundation — knowing what you stand for when everything is uncertain — is not just useful. In my experience, it's essential.

Faith gives me that foundation. It teaches humility: I don't have all the answers, and pretending otherwise is both dishonest and dangerous. It teaches resilience: setbacks are not the end of the story. And it teaches the value of community — the recognition that we are more capable together than any of us is alone.

I don't wear my faith as a label or use it to exclude. I do try to let it show in the texture of how I work: in listening more than I speak, in owning mistakes without hiding them, in celebrating others' successes more than my own, and in trying to do what I said I would do — every time.

If you want to explore any of these themes further — leadership, faith, purpose, or how they show up in the world of security and technology — I'd genuinely enjoy that conversation.
